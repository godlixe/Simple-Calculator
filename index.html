Simple Calculator

This calculator is made with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, all vanilla. I made this calculator as a practice for my JavaScript. This calculator uses stack to change infix operations into postfix and then evaluates it.

Why use stack? Computers don't know how to count using PEMDAS (parenthesis, exponents, multiplication, division, addition, subtraction), or simply the orders of counting. Given 4+2*3, the result will be 10 by using PEMDAS, and it all happens in our head. Us humans have intuition to determine the operator precedence/priority, but the computer doesn't. That's why stack is used. The operation is first converted from infix into postfix. Infix operations are the operations that we use daily, where the operators are inside the operand (4+2*3), and postfix operations have the operators after the operand (423*+). Convertion is needed as postfix operations does not need any parenthesis at all, it is already in order and is fast to be processed. The next step is just evaluating it by using basic operations.
